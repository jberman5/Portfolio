Center for Japanese Studies

A contextual-inquiry based consulting project

Project Overview

This project was focused on the use of the contextual-inquiry interview method as applied to a consulting model. Our team of four met with a client, conducted research and interviews, brainstormed solutions and ultimately provided recommendations designed to target their specified problem.

Deliverables

  • Presentation
  • Competitive Analysis
  • Affinity Wall
  • Recommendations

Background and Problem

The Center for Japanese Studies at University of Michigan was having trouble managing the Japanese Internship Initiative, a program that connects UM students with internships in Japan. The client communicated that the central problem was that the current proccess of diseminating information to students was manual and decentralized and there was no standad method for the CJS to receive studnet applications.

Research

Before conducting interviews our team generated a comprehensive background research report. I conducted a competitive analysis wherein I benchmarked the Center for Japanese Studies' program against other international internship programs. The analysis illuminated a number of potential strengths and weaknesses within the CJS that we would explore further.

After our background research report the team constructed a set of interview protocol for the stakeholders and conducted 5 interviews with former students, internship coordinators and company conatacts to better understand the current process and its pain points. After each interview our team came together to conduct interpertation sessions and generate affinity notes. Using the notes from our interpertation sessions we constructed an affinity wall and identified themes from our interviews.


Next Steps

Over the next few weeks we will be working to solidify our findings and we will then present recommendations to CJS. The project timeline is set to be completed by December 2017
